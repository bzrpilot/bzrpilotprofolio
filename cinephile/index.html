The Cinephile

"Cinema is not entertainment for me — it is lived experience."

Personal Philosophy

Lived Experience

I don't easily cry or react emotionally to real-life events. But cinema breaks through me. When emotions hit in a film, I don't just watch the story — I inhabit it. I live as the character.

Emotional Intelligence

Movies, series, and anime have shaped how I feel, think, and understand people. They are the mirror through which I view the complexity of human emotion.

Journey Into Cinema

The Awakening

My first English foreign language series was "13 Reasons Why". It wasn't just a show; it was a turning point that opened me to global storytelling.

The Anime Gateway

"Vinland Saga" was my first anime. It redefined my understanding of narrative depth, proving that animation is a powerful medium for mature, complex storytelling.

Cinema As Education

Stories became teachers where classrooms failed.

  • English & Foreign Languages
  • Dialogue & Communication
  • Human Psychology & Perspectives
  • Understanding Emotions

Watching English and foreign films improved my English more than formal education ever did. It taught me the nuance of language and conversation.

Love For The Theatre

The Dark Hall

The theatre is not just a place; it's a feeling. The silence, the sound, the giant screen—it creates the purest connection to the art. I often go alone or with friends, but the experience is always personal.

Series, Anime & Film

I have watched a large number of series, anime, and films. From grounded human dramas to intense anime narratives, storytelling across all formats deeply inspires me.
