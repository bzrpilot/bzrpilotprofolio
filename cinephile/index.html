The Cinephile

Cinema is not entertainment for me — it is lived experience.

Personal Cinema Philosophy

I don’t easily cry or react emotionally to real-life events, but cinema breaks through me. When emotions hit in a film, I live as the character. I don’t watch stories — I inhabit them.

Movies, series, and anime shape how I feel, think, and understand people. It's a bridge between my analytical nature and the visceral depth of human expression.

Journey Into Cinema

My journey into global storytelling began with turning points that shifted my perspective forever. The first foreign language series I ever watched was “13 Reasons Why”, and my introduction to anime was the visceral “Vinland Saga”.

These weren’t just shows; they were mirrors that opened me to global storytelling, culture, and a new spectrum of emotion.

Cinema as Education

Cinema has been my most profound teacher. Watching English and foreign films improved my English more than school or any formal education ever could. It helped me understand emotions, dialogue, perspectives, and the intricacies of human psychology.

In stories, I found lessons where classrooms failed, learning to decode the silent language of frames and the power of well-crafted dialogue.

Love for the Theatre

I prefer watching movies in theatres, often going alone or with friends. The dark hall, the intentional silence, the immersive sound, and the massive screen create the purest connection to a film.

Theatre is not just a place; it’s a feeling.

Series, Anime & Film Passion

Over the years, I have watched a large number of series, anime, and films. From grounded human dramas to intense anime narratives, storytelling across formats deeply inspires me and fuels my own creative execution.
