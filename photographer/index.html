The Photographer

"I don’t chase photos. I notice moments."

Mobile Philosophy

Instinct Over Setup

I shoot almost entirely on mobile. Photography for me is instinct, not setup. I capture what feels worth documenting, not what looks impressive.

Lived Moments

Many moments are lived fully before they are ever photographed. I prefer feeling the place over framing it.

Moments Over Perfection

I don’t shoot everything I see.

Sometimes I miss shots because I’m present in the moment. When I click, it’s because the moment asked to be remembered.

Subjects I Capture

Travel & Streets

Random scenes, places I pass through, and the unplanned visuals that carry quiet stories.

Architecture

Buildings and structures that stand as silent witnesses to time and history.

Portraits

Faces that tell a story without words, captured in their natural element.

Process & Introversion

Being introverted has made me miss opportunities to shoot more. I don’t always approach people or scenes comfortably.

I also have a large amount of unedited photo and video content. Many stories exist in raw form, waiting to be shaped.

Storytelling & Gear

Instagram Stories

I mostly share stories through Instagram. Highlights are used to preserve moments. Photography for me is about storytelling, not metrics or reach.

The Tool

Currently shooting on an iPhone 15 (base model). No professional claim — just a tool that stays with me everywhere.
