The Analyst

Curiosity came first. Skills followed.

Early Curiosity

My interest in cybersecurity began in childhood. I was always curious about computers and how they worked beyond the screen. It wasn't driven by hacking fantasies or a desire for mischief, but by a deep-seated need to understand systems at their core.

For me, the digital world was a giant puzzle, and I wanted to know how all the pieces fit together—and what happens when they don't.

The Accidental Entry (2019)

In 2019, while wandering around Calicut on one of my food hunting trips, I stumbled upon a free cybersecurity event. It was conducted by RedTeam Hacker Academy during its early days.

That event was a turning point. It opened my eyes to the real cyber world—beyond just curiosity and into a structured discipline. The people I met, the discussions about ethical hacking, and the collective mindset of the security community pushed me to dive deeper and commit to a formal path in cybersecurity.

Cybersecurity as a Discipline

Cybersecurity was never about quick money for me. Payment is earned by the value of work, not just the title. Even in a professional corporate setting, compensation is justified by the tangible impact of the security measures implemented.

For me, cyber work is both a profession and a hobby. It offers the unique freedom to work remotely, allowing me to travel the world and build businesses while maintaining a disciplined, professional career in security.

Projects & Hands-on Work

WebGuard 1.0 – Automated Web Vulnerability Scanner

2025
Python OWASP Top 10 Web Security

Built a Python-based modular web vulnerability scanner designed to automate the discovery of common web security flaws.

  • Identified OWASP Top 10 issues including SQL Injection, XSS, and missing security headers.
  • Validated efficacy using DVWA and OWASP Juice Shop.
  • Generated automated HTML vulnerability reports for documentation.
  • Gained hands-on experience in security testing and vulnerability reporting.

WebGuard v1.2 / v1.3 – Advanced Automated Scanner

2025
Python OWASP Top 10 VAPT Web App Security

Significant upgrades to the core scanner, enhancing detection capabilities and architectural modularity.

  • Added detection for IDOR, Open Redirect, and authentication flaws.
  • Refactored into a modular, plugin-based architecture for scalability.
  • Implemented color-coded terminal output for better readability.
  • Enhanced automated HTML reporting with detailed remediation advice.
  • Tested extensively against DVWA, OWASP Juice Shop, and testphp.vulnweb.com.
  • Strengthened both automated and manual VAPT skills.

Continuous Building

WebGuard is an evolving project. It reflects my journey from understanding systems to building tools that secure them. A final version with more advanced features, including AI-driven vulnerability suggestion and deeper integration with CI/CD pipelines, is planned.

The goal is constant learning, refinement, and maintaining real-world readiness in an ever-changing threat landscape.
